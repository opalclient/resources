## A current client versus a legacy specialist

Opal runs on MC 26.2 as a Fabric mod, the version being played right now. Slinky targets MC 1.7.10 and MC 1.8.9, the versions that defined early competitive soup PvP, and it gets there by injecting natively into the Minecraft process instead of loading as a mod. These two aren't really fighting for the same player. Opal plays the current game. Slinky plays the game a large slice of the ranked PvP scene never left, and to its credit, it has stayed actively updated there, with a public changelog and a Telegram feed to prove it.

## Price: one number is public, the other isn't

Opal costs $8.99, flat, lifetime, listed right on the page you're reading. Slinky is run as a registered commercial business and charges for its client, but its own documentation doesn't publish a price anywhere we could find. Maybe that's a business choice, maybe it's an oversight. Either way, only one of these clients you can budget for before you've talked to anyone.

## One client with two modes versus a pure ghost build

Opal ships a loud set tuned for BedWars and SkyWars-style combat and a quieter set built to hold up on most servers, in the same $8.99 download, plus an embedded scripting engine for anyone who wants to extend either side. Slinky skips the loud half entirely. Every module it ships, from Aim Assist to Auto Block to Auto Soup, is built around one goal: staying usable against anticheats and surviving a human reviewing a screenshare, down to modules rewritten to simulate legitimate inputs. If ghost-only is genuinely all you want, that single-minded focus is Slinky's real strength, and its resource-pack-style profile system for saving loadouts is a clean piece of design worth crediting on its own.

## Setup: a launcher versus an injector

Opal installs through a launcher: log in, launch, play. Slinky is a native loader that injects a DLL into the running Minecraft process, and that's also why a single build covers Vanilla, Forge, Lunar Client, and Badlion Client without separate versions for each, a genuine advantage of the native-injection model that Opal's Fabric-mod approach doesn't attempt. The tradeoff is a fixed, named binary sitting in memory: convenient right up until something learns its name.

## Platforms

Opal runs on Windows, macOS, and Linux. Slinky supports 64-bit Windows and Arch Linux, with no Mac build at all. If you're on a Mac, that decision has already been made for you.
