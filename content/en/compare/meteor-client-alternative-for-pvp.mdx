## What Meteor Client gets right

Credit where it's due: Meteor Client is a free, open-source anarchy client with a maintained Baritone fork and a large addon API, kept current on the newest Minecraft. For long-haul anarchy play and automation, it's one of the best tools out there, and a paid client doesn't replace what it does. If that's the job you need done, Meteor is a genuinely strong choice.

## A different job: minigame PvP

Meteor is anarchy-first and makes no bypass claims, so BedWars and SkyWars-style minigame combat was never really the assignment. That's the gap Opal fills. Opal draws its own glass UI through NanoVG, ships 70 modules tuned out of the box, and runs a hybrid set: the loud half (flight, scaffold, aggressive combat) for minigame fights, the quiet half (subtle aim, reach, velocity) for most other servers. If you want combat that's already tuned before you've opened a single settings panel, that's Opal's lane, for $8.99.

## The other free swaps

If free is the requirement and Meteor's particular flavor of anarchy isn't quite it, there are two more worth knowing about:

- **Wurst**: the longest-running readable open-source anarchy client, still free and still updated. If you want to actually read the code you're running, this is the one with the longest track record of people doing exactly that.
- **LiquidBounce**: free, open source, and scriptable, and the one free client here that also ships named bypass profiles, so it reaches further toward anticheated play than Meteor or Wurst do.

## Which alternative should you actually pick?

- **Still want free anarchy and Baritone?** Meteor Client or Wurst.
- **Free, scriptable, and reaching toward bypass territory?** LiquidBounce.
- **Polished minigame PvP with a glass interface built specifically for it?** Opal.

If you outgrow minigame combat entirely and want dedicated depth across more servers, that's what Terminus is built for.
