## Platform

This is where Opal pulls ahead significantly. Rise ships a JAR and has a Discord server. That's it.

Opal comes with:
- **Desktop launcher** — handles installation, updates, and mod management
- **Web dashboard** — account management, HWID resets, downloads, changelogs
- **Config sharing** — browse, publish, and download community configs
- **Community forums** — bug reports, suggestions, roadmap, changelog
- **Discord bot** — license management, support, and community features

## Scripting

Both clients offer scripting, but the implementations differ. Opal uses a **GraalVM JavaScript engine** — a full JS runtime with thread-safe bindings to the client's internal APIs. This means you can write complex automation scripts, custom modules, and integrations using familiar JavaScript.

## Version Support

Rise supports 1.8.9, 1.12.2, and 1.19.4. Opal targets **Minecraft 1.21.10** via Fabric, with compatibility for both 1.8 and latest-version servers. If you play on modern Minecraft, Opal has you covered.

## The Verdict

Rise is a solid blatant client with a large community, but at $35 it's hard to justify when Opal offers **more features, ghost capabilities, and a full platform ecosystem at $8.99**.

If you need ghost features, Rise simply doesn't have them. If you want blatant server bypasses, both clients deliver — but Opal does it at a quarter of the price with a better overall experience.
