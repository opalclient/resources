## What free actually gets you

The free open source clients in this comparison are real, full projects, not toys. LiquidBounce ships scripting and named bypass profiles for free. Meteor Client and Wurst are mature, actively maintained, and updated for current Minecraft. Because the code is public, you can read exactly what all three of them do before you ever run them. If your priorities are zero cost, full auditability, and you genuinely don't mind wiring up your own setup, free is the right answer here, and we're not going to try to talk you out of it.

## What $8.99 actually buys

Opal isn't selling you a capability that doesn't already exist somewhere for free. It's selling you a finished product. You get a custom glass UI engine with an actual design language behind it, 70 modules tuned out of the box, and a launcher that logs you in and gets you playing. The module set covers BedWars/SkyWars-style minigame combat and a quieter set for most other servers, so you're not stitching two setups together yourself. For the price of a sandwich, you skip the config session and start with something that already looks and feels done. "Customizable" can do a lot of work in a product description. The honest translation, for a from-scratch free build, is an evening in config files before it feels like yours.

## Say what you're doing

Opal doesn't hijack your referral links or run quiet tricks behind your back. What it does, you can see. What it opts you into, you opted into. That's a low bar. It's also a bar a lot of paid clients in this space don't clear. Part of what your $8.99 buys is a name behind the price tag that you can actually trust.
