## Where Silhouette earns it

We're not going to pretend Silhouette is a pushover. It's one of a small handful of paid clients building seriously for MC 1.21.11, and its per-anticheat bypass tuning isn't a bullet point bolted onto a features page, it's the actual core of the product. The repository-based account manager with built-in ban checks is a genuinely unusual feature too: most utility clients treat account handling as an afterthought, and Silhouette built a whole workflow around it. If your priority is that specific account tooling and tight per-anticheat tuning, Silhouette is a fair, honest pick.

## What Opal does better

Opal competes on the thing you notice every second you're actually playing: polish. The interface runs on a custom glass UI engine, with a real design language behind it rather than a menu that looks bolted on. The module set is broad, 70 of them spanning combat, movement, visual, world, and utility, and it's tuned out of the box, so you're not spending your first evening in a config file before you can play a game. At $8.99 for a lifetime license, it's also the easier decision.

## One client, two jobs

Opal is built as a hybrid. The loud set, flight, scaffold, aggressive combat, is tuned for BedWars and SkyWars-style minigame fights. The quiet set, subtler aim assistance, reach, velocity, is built to stay calm on most servers. You buy one client and switch depending on where you're playing. Silhouette is the more single-minded tool: its whole design center of gravity is competitive ranked PvP, and its bypass tuning is built around that fight, not a second one.
