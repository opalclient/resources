## Where Augustus earns its reputation

Augustus built its name on depth. Around 209 modules and a settings tree that runs into the tens of thousands mean you can tune almost any value to a specific number, and for the 1.8 combat era, still the competitive PvP standard for a lot of players, that is a genuinely serious toolset. If you live on 1.8.9 and want to micro-adjust everything under the hood, Augustus is a fair, well earned pick.

## Where Opal picks up

Opal is built for where Minecraft is now, not where it was over a decade ago. It runs on MC 26.2, draws its own glass interface through NanoVG, and ships 70 features tuned out of the box, so you are playing within seconds instead of reading a settings tree before your first match. "Deeply configurable" is a real compliment for Augustus. It is also, honestly, a description of an evening you will spend in menus before you ever load into a game. Opal skips that evening.

## One client, two jobs

Opal is a hybrid. The loud set, flight, scaffold, aggressive combat, is built for BedWars and SkyWars-style minigame fights. The quiet set, subtle aim, reach, velocity, is built to stay calm on most servers. Augustus is focused squarely on 1.8.9 combat. Opal covers minigame combat and most-server play on a current version instead.
