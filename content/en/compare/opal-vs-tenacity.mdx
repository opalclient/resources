## Actively maintained vs abandoned since 2024

Tenacity's own record marks it abandoned, and it was sold through intent.store across 2020 to 2024. Whatever it cost back then, you cannot buy it today, and nobody is shipping fixes for it against anything current.

Opal is under active development for MC 26.2 right now, distributed through a launcher that handles login and updates for you. $8.99 buys a client someone is still patching, not a snapshot of a client from a marketplace that closed its doors.

## MC 26.2 vs a client frozen on 1.8.9

Tenacity never left MC 1.8.9. That was the right call for its era, and the client leaned into it hard, down to a ViaLoadingBase wrapper just to let an old client talk to newer servers. It is still a 1.8.9 client, full stop.

Opal runs on MC 26.2 as a Fabric mod. If you are playing on the version people actually run today, Tenacity is not an option, no matter how deep its 1.8.9 module set went.

## Module count: 86 vs 70, and what is actually in each pile

Tenacity's 86 modules span combat, movement, player, and exploit, plus a render category alone that runs to roughly 32 entries, several of which are reskins of the same TargetHUD overlay in different visual themes rather than distinct functionality. It is a genuinely large roster for a 1.8.9-era client, and that earns real credit.

Opal's 70 modules split across combat, movement, visual, world, and utility, built for MC 26.2 rather than carried forward from an older codebase. Fewer total modules, sure. None of them are a fourth coat of paint on the same HUD.

## Two clients, one bet on scripting, a decade apart

Tenacity shipped its own in-client JavaScript scripting engine back when that was unusual for a paid utility client, letting users extend it without recompiling. That was a genuinely forward-looking call for its era, and it deserves to be remembered as one.

Opal takes the same bet further with an embedded GraalJS runtime and a proxy surface covering player, render, rotation, world, and module state, plus a GraalJS/Yoga UI layer built on top of it. Tenacity got there first with the idea. Opal is doing more with it on tooling that did not exist yet when Tenacity shipped.

## When Tenacity is the better call

Tenacity and Opal are not really competing for the same buyer right now. Tenacity served MC 1.8.9 PvP players through intent.store during a marketplace era that has since closed out, and it is not a live purchase option anymore. Still, there are honest reasons to reach for it.
