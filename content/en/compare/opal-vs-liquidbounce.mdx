## Free vs $8.99: what the price actually buys

LiquidBounce is free and open source, and that is a real selling point, not a consolation prize. If you want zero cost, want to read the source yourself, or actually enjoy wiring up your own setup, it is a legitimately strong pick and we are not going to pretend otherwise.

Opal costs $8.99, once. What that buys you is a finished product instead of a toolkit: a launcher that logs you in and drops you straight into the game, an interface with an actual design language behind it instead of a stock settings panel, and a module set that is already tuned before you have opened a single config panel. You are not paying for features LiquidBounce lacks. You are paying to skip the part where you build the client yourself.

## The interface

This is the easiest place to see the gap. Opal draws its own glass UI through a custom engine built on Minecraft's rendering pipeline, with an actual design language behind it: a runtime theme, a proper accent system, type that was chosen rather than defaulted to. LiquidBounce renders its menu as a web page inside the game, which is genuinely flexible if you want to restyle it yourself, but it reads exactly like what it is. If how the client looks while you play matters to you, this one is not close.

## One client, two jobs

Opal is built to do two jobs, not one. The loud set, flight, scaffold, aggressive combat, is tuned for BedWars/SkyWars-style minigame fights. The quiet set, built around subtle aim assist, is built to sit calmly on most servers instead of announcing itself. You are not buying two clients and picking one at checkout. You are buying one that already knows the difference and switches with you. LiquidBounce can be pushed toward either playstyle too, you just do the tuning.

## What you see is what it does

Opal does not quietly hijack referral links or run tricks behind the scenes you never agreed to. What it does, you can see. What it opts you into, you opted into. That sounds like a low bar to clear. It is still worth saying out loud, and it is part of why the name is trusted.
