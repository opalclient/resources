## Free vs $8.99: what the price actually buys

LiquidBounce is free and open-source, and that is a real selling point. If you want zero cost, want to read the code, or like wiring up your own setup, it is a strong pick and we are not going to pretend otherwise.

Opal costs $8.99. What you get for it is a finished product instead of a toolkit: a launcher that logs you in and gets you in-game, a UI that does not look like a config menu from 2015, and a feature set that is tuned out of the box. You pay to skip the setup and the guesswork.

## The interface

This is the easiest place to see the gap. Opal draws its own glass UI through NanoVG, with a real design language behind it: a runtime theme, a proper accent system, clean type. LiquidBounce renders its menu as a web page inside the game, which is flexible but feels like what it is. If you care how the client looks while you play, Opal is hard to beat.

## One client, two jobs

Opal is a hybrid. The loud set, flight, scaffold, aggressive combat, is built for BedWars and SkyWars-style minigame fights. The quiet set, subtle aim, reach, velocity, is built to stay calm on most servers. You do not buy two clients. You buy one that does both, and switch depending on where you are playing. LiquidBounce can be pushed toward either, but you do the tuning.
