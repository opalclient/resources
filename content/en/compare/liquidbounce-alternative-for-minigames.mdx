## Why look for a LiquidBounce alternative

LiquidBounce is a genuinely strong free client: open source, well documented, and it does what it does well for anarchy and general play. Its natural lane just is not BedWars or SkyWars-style minigame combat. If that is what you actually play, the search for an alternative usually comes down to one of two things: you want to stay free and try a different flavor, or you want something that already feels tuned for minigames the moment you open it.

## The free, open-source alternatives

If your reason for wanting an alternative is cost, stay free. Meteor Client is the broad modern anarchy toolkit, complete with a bundled Baritone fork for anyone who would rather automate the walking than do it themselves. Wurst is the longest-running readable open client around, for players who want years of maturity baked into the code they are reading. Raven B++ is a free, open-source pick in the 1.8.9 lineage with minigame helpers built in. All three are genuinely good, all three cost nothing, and we are not going to pretend otherwise.

## The polished paid alternative: Opal

What none of the free clients hand you is a finished, designed product. Opal draws its own glass UI through NanoVG, ships 70 modules tuned out of the box, and is built as a hybrid: the loud set (flight, scaffold, aggressive combat) for BedWars and SkyWars-style fights, the quiet set (subtle aim, reach, velocity) for most servers. LiquidBounce's browser-rendered menu is flexible if restyling a webpage is your idea of a good evening, but it reads plainer, and its center of gravity is anarchy and general play, not minigames. If the polished minigame experience is what you are after, that is Opal's lane, for $8.99.
