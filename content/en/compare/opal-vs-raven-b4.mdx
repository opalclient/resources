## A build in a lineage, not a full feature list

Raven B4's own record is short: it's the successor to Raven B3 in blowsy's Raven line, explicitly called out as unrelated to Raven B++, a different client by K-ov that just happens to share part of the name. That disambiguation is worth knowing if you're searching around Raven-branded clients. B4 and B++ are different codebases, not a fork of one another.

Opal is documented in a lot more depth: pricing, a full module breakdown, a UI stack, and a scripting engine all live in its own record. That gap isn't a knock on B4's engineering. It's just what survived into the record for this particular build.

## A Forge mod from a much older version vs a client built for today's release

Raven B4 runs on Forge for MC 1.8.9, loaded as a mod rather than through any external injector. Opal runs on Fabric for MC 26.2, the version shipping right now. These aren't really two clients competing for the same lobby. One only works on a Minecraft release from over a decade ago, the other only works on the one you're playing today. If you're not already on 1.8.9, Raven B4 isn't a worse option, it's not an option at all.

## Active development vs a legacy build with no update trail

Opal carries a status of active: it's still being built, its module count is current as of its own last update, and it ships through a maintained launcher. Raven B4 carries a status of legacy. There's no build recorded after B4, no price, no ongoing support, just its place in the lineage right after Raven B3.

## Installing a jar yourself vs a launcher that does it for you

Getting Raven B4 running means installing it as a Forge mod yourself, the standard manual path for an older client on an older loader. Opal ships through its own launcher: you log in and it handles the rest. Call the manual route "hands-on" if you like the sound of it. The honest translation is one more evening spent finding the right mods folder before you've actually played a game.

## What Raven B4 actually has going for it

If you're still playing on MC 1.8.9, that alone rules Opal out for you, full stop. Opal doesn't run there. And being the documented successor to Raven B3 in a specific, named lineage is a real thing, not nothing: it means B4 is a traceable step in a client's history, not a random anonymous jar someone uploaded once. That's a genuinely narrower audience than Opal's, which is built for players on the current release who want a finished, supported product out of the box.
