<KeyTakeaways>
- Lightning Tracker is a world-awareness module that marks every lightning strike the moment it lands, even ones outside your field of view.
- It's used to locate trial chambers, ancient cities, and other builds that run on lightning rods or lightning-triggered redstone.
- It only surfaces information vanilla Minecraft already broadcasts to nearby players, so it carries essentially no anticheat risk.
- Tenacity is documented as a client that ships it.
</KeyTakeaways>

Lightning Tracker is a world-awareness module that catches every lightning strike in the loaded world and marks it the instant it happens, instead of leaving you to catch the flash and thunder yourself. Vanilla Minecraft already renders every bolt for anyone standing nearby: the module doesn't add new information, it just makes sure a strike happening behind you, off to the side, or three chunks away doesn't slip past unnoticed. That's the whole pitch. No new packets, no new textures, just a nudge toward something the game was already showing you.

## How it works

The module hooks into the game's lightning strike events and drops a marker the moment a bolt lands, whether you're looking at it or not. Minecraft already spawns a lightning entity with a visible flash and audible thunder for every player in render distance, so the module isn't intercepting anything hidden from other players, isn't predicting where a bolt will land, and isn't creating strikes that wouldn't otherwise happen. It's closer to a highlighter than a wallhack: it circles what's already on the page.

That distinction is what makes it useful. Trial chambers, ancient cities, and a fair number of custom redstone builds lean on lightning rods or lightning-triggered contraptions as part of their design. Walking a region on foot hoping to catch a strike out of the corner of your eye is slow, and easy to miss entirely. Running the module while you explore turns every strike, including the ones you'd normally walk right past, into a pointer toward a structure you'd otherwise find by luck.

## Is it safe to use

Short answer: yes. Lightning Tracker doesn't touch anything the server cares about. It doesn't move your character, doesn't change what packets your client sends, and doesn't alter hit detection the way a combat module like [Killaura](/en/learn/what-is-killaura) does, or send you flying the way a movement module like Flight does. Every strike it marks is already broadcast to every client in range, so the module is reading data your game already has rather than asking the server for anything extra.

Server-side anticheats look for the fingerprints of an unfair advantage: impossible movement, suspicious swing timing, position desync. None of that applies here. It behaves more like a rendering convenience than a gameplay edge, the kind of feature you could explain to a server admin without flinching.
