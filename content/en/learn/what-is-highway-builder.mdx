Highway Builder is a world module that automatically constructs a long, straight path across terrain, laying blocks under your feet and clearing whatever stands in the way as you move. Instead of manually bridging gaps, digging through hills, and chopping every tree between you and where you're going, you point yourself in a direction and let the module handle construction. A route that would take an hour of hand-placed blocks becomes something you build by holding a movement key.

## How it works

Highway Builder runs while you walk or fly in a chosen direction. Each tick, it places blocks ahead of and beneath you to keep the path solid and level, and it breaks or bypasses whatever terrain, trees, or other obstacles sit in the route. The result is a continuous, walkable (and often ridable) surface running in a straight line, rather than one that follows the natural contour of the land the way a normal path would.

It's documented in Meteor Client, the client tracked for this module. The module doesn't design a route for you; it just executes one, tick by tick, in whatever direction you're already facing or flying.

Long-distance travel in vanilla survival is slow. Terrain, water, and elevation changes all break up movement, and placing a path by hand across hundreds or thousands of blocks is tedious work. Highway Builder automates that construction so you can lay a route to a base, a trade hub, or a border once and reuse it, or build one on the fly while already traveling. On anarchy servers especially, where infrastructure like nether portals or established roads is scarce or gets destroyed on sight, a self-built highway is a practical way to get somewhere quickly and repeatably.

## Is it safe to use

Highway Builder is a world-building convenience, not a combat or movement exploit. It places and breaks blocks the same way you could by hand, just automated and faster. It doesn't alter combat outcomes, movement speed limits, or anything a server-side anticheat typically watches for in the way combat or movement cheats do.

The practical risk sits with server rules, not detection. Some servers restrict automated building or terrain modification outright, and rapid, uniform block placement running in a straight line for hundreds of blocks can be visually obvious to other players or staff even where nothing flags it automatically. Whether it's acceptable comes down to the individual server's policy on automation, not any inherent anticheat signature.

Opal doesn't ship a Highway Builder equivalent. Its module lineup leans combat, movement, and visual: the edges you feel mid-fight, not infrastructure you build once and reuse. If cross-map travel automation is what you're after, it isn't currently part of what Opal ships.
