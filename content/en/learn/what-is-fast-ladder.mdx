Fast Ladder is a movement module that increases how quickly a player climbs ladders and vines beyond what vanilla Minecraft allows. It only kicks in while the player is attached to a ladder or vine block, letting them scale a tower, a mineshaft wall, or a vine column faster than an unmodified client permits. Nothing else about movement changes. Walk away from the ladder and you're back to normal speed.

## How Fast Ladder works

Vanilla Minecraft caps vertical speed on a climbable block, no matter how hard you hold the movement key. It doesn't matter if you're sprinting, jumping, or spamming forward: the climb rate is fixed. Fast Ladder overrides that cap. Holding forward against a ladder or vine moves the player up (or down) faster than the client would otherwise allow.

That's the whole mechanic. It isn't a new movement type stacked on top of climbing, like a dash or a boosted jump. It's the existing climb action running at a rate the game was never built to produce.

## Why people actually use it

The pitch is time, not combat. Fast Ladder has zero effect on the ground or in open air, so it's closer to a convenience feature than a fight-winning one. Survival builds, towers, mineshafts, and vine-choked terrain all involve long vertical stretches, and cutting the climb time down is the entire point. It's documented in clients like Fan and Tenacity, where players use it to get through the vertical sections of a build without sitting through vanilla's climb rate.

## Is it safe to use

Fast Ladder still moves a player faster than vanilla physics allow, which puts it in movement-advantage territory rather than purely cosmetic territory. A server-side anticheat that tracks velocity can compare observed climb speed against the known vanilla ladder and vine rate, and flag movement that consistently beats it.

The risk profile is lower than something like a bunny-hop or a flat sprint-speed module, mainly because the window is small. Climbing only applies while the player is physically attached to a ladder or vine, so there's less time for an anticheat to sample the anomaly compared to a module running continuously across open ground. Smaller window doesn't mean invisible window. It's still a measurable deviation from vanilla movement, and that's exactly the category anticheats are built to catch.
