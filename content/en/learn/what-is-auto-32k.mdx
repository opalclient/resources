Auto 32k is an exploit module that automates the "32k" enchanted-sword damage exploit, a piece of Minecraft 1.12.2-era anarchy server lore where a stacked-enchantment weapon deals damage nowhere near what vanilla survival rules allow. The name refers to the exploit itself, not a setting or a threshold you tune. The module just removes the manual labor of reproducing it.

## How the underlying exploit works

The exploit comes from abusing enchantment stacking: a weapon ends up carrying far more enchantment power than a single sword is supposed to hold under normal survival rules. A vanilla enchanted sword deals a modest, predictable bump in damage. The stacked version doesn't. It deals damage many times higher on a single hit, the kind of number that has no legitimate in-game explanation.

Auto 32k doesn't invent a new trick on top of that. It automates the existing one. Instead of a player working through the steps by hand to produce an overpowered sword, the module runs the process for them so it can be repeated on demand. It's documented as an exploit-category module in SalHack, a 1.12.2 anarchy and crystal-combat client built around exactly this kind of no-rules PvP.

The appeal is straightforward. Anarchy servers settle fights on raw combat power, not moderation, and a sword that one-shots people wins outright. A hit that would normally chip a sliver off someone's health bar instead ends the fight before it starts.

## Is it safe to use

No, for two separate reasons, and neither depends on which anticheat a server runs.

First, it's fragile by design. The exploit only works where the 1.12.2-era enchantment-stacking bug still exists. Patch the bug, update the server, and Auto 32k stops producing anything unusual. It's not a general combat advantage, it's a very specific, very dated hole that most modern environments have already closed.

Second, when it does work, it's the opposite of subtle. Reach is a few extra blocks nobody notices mid-fight. A sword doing multiples of normal damage on a single swing is immediately obvious to the person on the receiving end, and to anyone watching the fight play out. Automating a known damage bug also counts as bug abuse under the rules of essentially any server that bothers to have rules, independent of whatever detection stack is or isn't watching.

<Callout variant="warning" title="Bug abuse, not a combat edge">
Auto 32k automates a documented server-side exploit rather than a client-side rendering or prediction trick. Running it is bug abuse on any server that prohibits exploiting bugs, and the damage output makes it unmistakable to opponents even before staff get involved.
</Callout>

Opal doesn't ship anything like Auto 32k, and that's not an oversight.
