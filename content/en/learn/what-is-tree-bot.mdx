<KeyTakeaways>

- Tree-bot is a Minecraft module that fells trees automatically: it walks to the nearest log, breaks every connected block in the trunk, and moves on to the next tree.
- Most implementations replant a sapling on the stump right after cutting, so the tree regrows without any manual planting.
- It's a Wurst automation module, not a combat or movement cheat. No aiming, clicking, or steering required.
- Detection risk from anticheat is low since it never touches reach, rotations, or hit registration. The real risk is server rules against automation itself.

</KeyTakeaways>

Tree-bot is a Minecraft module that chops down trees on its own: find the log, walk to it, break the whole trunk, replant, repeat. It's built for wood farming, the part of the game where you'd otherwise be standing in a forest holding left click for ten minutes straight. Nothing about it touches PvP or movement. It's a chore-killer, not an edge finder.

## How it works

Tree-bot scans the area around the player for log blocks once it's switched on. When it finds one, it walks the player to the base of the tree and breaks the trunk upward through every connected log, including the diagonal or leaning growth patterns certain tree types produce. Clear one tree and it moves straight to the next one in range, repeating the same loop for as long as trees are left standing nearby.

Replanting is the second half of the cycle. Most builds place a sapling on the stump the moment the trunk comes down, so instead of walking back to a bare patch of dirt later, the player finds a tree already regrowing. The player isn't aiming, clicking, or steering any of it. Tree-bot owns the pathing, the breaking, and the placing from start to finish.

The appeal is obvious if you've ever farmed wood for a build or a shop restock by hand: it's repetitive and it eats time you could spend doing literally anything else. Tree-bot turns "go chop wood" into "start Tree-bot, come back to a stack of logs." It's documented in Wurst sitting next to other utility and automation modules, not the combat ones, which tells you exactly what bucket it belongs in.

## Is it safe to use

Tree-bot doesn't touch reach, rotations, or hit registration, the exact mechanics most combat anticheats are built to police, so it doesn't trip the checks written to catch killaura or reach hacks. Breaking a log and placing a sapling aren't superhuman actions on their own; a server watching packet timing for combat exploits has nothing to flag here.

The real exposure is automation itself, independent of anything an anticheat can see. A lot of servers ban any client-side macro or bot behavior that acts without direct player input, full stop, and wood farming doesn't get a pass just because it's harmless to everyone else in the world. A server with that kind of blanket rule treats Tree-bot as botting no matter how clean it looks in practice. A server that only writes rules around combat and movement exploits is far less likely to care what's chopping your trees.
