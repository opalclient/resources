- It only upgrades, never downgrades: equipped armor stays on until something stronger arrives.

Auto Armor is a module in Minecraft utility clients that keeps your armor slots filled with the strongest gear you own, without you ever opening your inventory. Instead of pausing mid-fight to compare a fresh chestplate against the one you're wearing, the module runs that comparison constantly and slots the better piece in the moment it's worth wearing. It's not going to win a fight for you. It just makes sure you're never caught in weaker armor than what's already in your pack.

## How it works

Auto Armor watches two things: your inventory and your four armor slots (helmet, chestplate, leggings, boots). Whenever a new item lands in your inventory, or on a regular check, it compares whatever's sitting in each slot against the armor you're carrying and ranks everything by protection value.

If a carried piece outranks what's equipped, the module moves it into place. Mechanically that's the same inventory-to-armor-slot transfer the game already lets you trigger by hand, a shift-click or a drag-and-drop into the armor UI. Auto Armor just fires that transfer the instant a better piece exists instead of waiting for you to notice.

## Why bother automating it

Picking up a stronger piece during a fight and forgetting to actually put it on is an easy, very human mistake. You're busy not dying, not auditing four armor slots. Auto Armor removes that failure mode: whatever's best goes on immediately, whether you're paying attention or not.

It's usually grouped with the other convenience-focused player modules rather than combat or movement tech. Fan and Tenacity both document versions of it.

## Is it safe to use

Auto Armor sits closer to a convenience feature than a mechanical cheat. Equipping armor from your inventory is something every player is already allowed to do by hand, and the module doesn't touch reach, hit registration, movement speed, or anything else a server can measure as physically impossible. It's automating a legal action, not inventing a new one.

That said, it's still use-at-your-own-risk. An automated swap happens faster and more consistently than a person dragging items around, and some anticheats flag any pattern of instant, repeated inventory manipulation on principle, regardless of what the action accomplishes. Whether a given server catches it depends more on how closely it watches inventory actions than on anything Auto Armor does wrong.

</Callout>
