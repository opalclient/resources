<KeyTakeaways>

- TriggerBot is a combat module that auto-fires the instant your crosshair lands on a valid target, no clicking needed.
- It never moves your aim, that part stays on you, unlike an aimbot.
- It's narrower than KillAura, which aims and attacks on its own; TriggerBot only automates the click.
- Anticheats mostly catch it through click-timing patterns, not aim rotation.

</KeyTakeaways>

TriggerBot is a Minecraft combat module that swings for you. The moment your crosshair passes over a valid target, whether that's another player or a mob, it fires the attack automatically, the same packet a manual left-click would send. You still have to aim. TriggerBot just removes the reaction time between finding the target and hitting it. That's a narrower job than people assume when they hear "bot" in the name, and it's exactly what separates TriggerBot from an aimbot, which does the aiming, and from KillAura, which does both.

## How TriggerBot works

Every tick, TriggerBot checks what's sitting directly under your crosshair. If it's a valid target (another player, a mob, whatever the configuration allows) the module fires the same attack packet a manual click would send. There's no scanning the room and no picking the best of three players in your field of view. It watches exactly one spot: dead center of your screen.

That narrowness is the whole point. Aiming stays entirely on you. Move your crosshair off the target and TriggerBot goes quiet, nothing fires until you're looking at something valid again. Compare that to KillAura, which finds targets on its own and turns your view toward them without any input from you. TriggerBot removes exactly one step: the click.

## TriggerBot vs KillAura vs aimbot

These three combat modules automate different pieces of the same fight, which is why people mix them up, especially since clients often bundle all three together.

| Module | Aims for you | Attacks for you |
|---|---|---|
| Aimbot | Yes | No, you still click |
| KillAura | Yes | Yes, both aiming and attacking |
| TriggerBot | No | Yes, only the click is automated |

TriggerBot sits at the narrow end of that table. It doesn't select targets or rotate your view the way [KillAura](/en/learn/what-is-killaura) does, and it doesn't align your crosshair the way an [aimbot](/en/learn/what-is-an-aimbot) does. All it strips out is the gap between your crosshair landing on someone and the hit registering.

## Why it's harder to catch

TriggerBot still automates a real gameplay action rather than just displaying information, so it counts as a gameplay-advantage cheat, not a cosmetic convenience. What makes it quieter than KillAura is exactly what it doesn't do: no rotation to inspect, no reach to measure, because you were already looking at the target when it fired.

The signal an anticheat actually has to work with is timing. A real click and the moment a crosshair lands on target vary with whatever the player's reaction time is that day. TriggerBot can fire with a consistency no human reproduces, and click patterns that stay too fast or too uniform across many fights are exactly what timing-based checks look for. It's quieter than KillAura or an aimbot. It isn't invisible, since the attack packets still have to clear the same server-side validation as any other hit.
