<KeyTakeaways>

- Burrow is a combat module that digs a player into a block and seals them inside, cutting off melee's line of attack.
- It's a panic button, not a weapon: a few seconds to heal or retreat from a losing fight.
- Documented in Meteor Client's combat category, it blocks melee only, not suffocation, fall damage, or ranged attacks.

</KeyTakeaways>

Burrow is a combat module that digs a player into a block and seals them inside it, cutting off the direct line an opponent's melee attack needs to connect. Instead of trading hits, the player vanishes into solid stone for a few seconds: no gap to swing through, no fight happening. It's built as a defensive move, not an offensive one. Someone losing a fight, sitting on low health, or caught without an escape route triggers it to buy a short window, enough time to eat, let regeneration catch up, or simply break the engagement before deciding whether to come back out swinging.

## How it works

Burrow places or breaks blocks around the player until they're closed in on every side, floor, ceiling, walls, with no opening an attack can pass through. Minecraft's melee combat depends on a clear line between attacker and target, so removing that line removes the fight entirely.

Inside the seal, the player isn't doing anything offensive. They're just waiting: letting health regenerate, letting the fight outside cool off, or timing their exit. When they're ready, they break back out and the fight picks up again on their terms, not their opponent's.

It's documented as seen in Meteor Client, filed under its combat category alongside modules that fight rather than hide, which makes Burrow the odd one out there: a combat module built entirely around not fighting.

## Why people use it

The appeal isn't damage, it's time. A player who's losing a fight, sitting on half a heart, or caught out with no escape route can trigger Burrow to remove themselves from danger for a handful of seconds. That's enough to eat, let natural regeneration catch up, or reset the engagement before choosing whether to reopen it.

It's a panic button, not a strategy for winning. Nobody burrows their way to a kill. They burrow their way out of one.

## Is it safe to use

Burrow doesn't fake anything the server has to catch. It isn't spoofing aim, timing, or movement packets, it's placing and breaking real blocks, an action the game already expects a player to take. That puts it closer to a tactical trick than a hidden statistical edge, and it's a large part of why it reads differently from a module that fabricates hits.

That said, digging yourself into a hole mid-fight and popping back out seconds later is not normal play. Opponents notice. Staff reviewing a clip notice. Servers that restrict block placement or breaking during combat will flag the pattern immediately, and even where there's no rule written specifically against it, it's an easy thing to get questioned over.

<Callout variant="tip" title="Where Opal draws the line">
Opal doesn't ship a Burrow-style panic seal. Digging a hole to survive a fight that was already lost treats the symptom, not the cause, and it's a strange enough pattern that it draws exactly the kind of attention a combat module should be built to avoid. Opal's combat modules exist, but the goal is staying in a fight long enough to win it, not disappearing from one.
</Callout>

</Callout>

## FAQ

<FAQList>

<FAQItem question="Does Burrow make you invincible in Minecraft?">
No. Burrow blocks direct melee attacks by sealing you inside a block, but it does nothing against suffocation, fall damage, or ranged and area attacks that don't need a clear line to hit you.
</FAQItem>

<FAQItem question="Is Burrow the same as KillAura or Reach?">
No. KillAura and Reach are offensive modules that automate or extend attacks against an opponent. Burrow doesn't attack anyone, it just seals the user inside a block defensively, which puts it in a different category entirely.
</FAQItem>

<FAQItem question="Which Minecraft client has the Burrow module?">
Burrow is documented as seen in Meteor Client, filed under its combat category alongside other defensive and offensive tools.
</FAQItem>

<FAQItem question="Can you get banned for using Burrow?">
Any module bundled inside a hacked client can get an account banned simply for being part of that client. Burrow itself doesn't fabricate hits or movement the way an aim or reach cheat does, so its detection profile is different, but running it still means running a hacked client.
</FAQItem>
