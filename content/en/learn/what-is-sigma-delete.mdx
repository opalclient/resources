SigmaDelete is an exploit module in Minecraft utility clients that empties a player's inventory almost instantly, well before anyone could manually drag items out one slot at a time or drop them individually. It's named after the Sigma client lineage, where the trick first circulated before spreading to other clients, including Fan. The point isn't combat or movement. It's timing: getting rid of incriminating items before a screenshare, an admin, or anyone else gets a look at what's in the bag.

## How SigmaDelete works

Clearing an inventory by hand in survival means dragging items out of their slots or dropping them one by one, and both take several seconds. That gap is a problem if someone is watching, because it leaves a window where an observer can see exactly what a player is holding before it's gone. SigmaDelete collapses that whole process into a single action. Items that would normally take a noticeable stretch of time to remove disappear at once, with nothing resembling an ordinary drop or crafting sequence to explain where they went.

The name comes from the Sigma client lineage, where similar rapid-clear tricks first showed up before the technique spread to other clients under the same idea. It's documented as seen in Fan.

None of this touches how a player fights or moves. SigmaDelete doesn't extend reach, add speed, or aim anything. It exists purely to control what an inventory shows at the exact moment someone else looks at it, which is why it sits in the exploit category rather than combat or movement.

## Is it safe to use

No. SigmaDelete isn't a quality-of-life convenience, it's built to evade detection, and running it carries its own risk on top of whatever it's being used to hide. A complete inventory clear that happens in an instant, right before or during a screenshare or inspection, is itself a pattern reviewers watch for. An inventory that empties with no matching drop or crafting action to explain it reads as evasive even to someone who never saw what was inside it.

It also doesn't do what people hope it does. A server can log inventory contents and changes independently of whatever the client displays on screen, so clearing items client-side is no guarantee the server-side record agrees. Leaning on SigmaDelete to erase proof of other cheating doesn't undo whatever was already flagged before the clear happened.

<Callout variant="tip" title="Where Opal draws the line">
Opal doesn't ship a module called SigmaDelete, and that's not an oversight. An instant, evidence-clearing inventory dump isn't a combat or movement feature, it's a tool built around getting caught doing something else. Opal's own catalog stays in combat, movement, and visual utility: modules that change how you play, not ones built to make an inspection find nothing.
</Callout>
