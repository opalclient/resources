China Hat is a cosmetic module in Minecraft utility clients, most notably documented as a feature of Tenacity, that draws a decorative hat-shaped model or overlay on your own screen. It lives in the visual category, the same bucket as skins, capes, and HUD tweaks: things that change what you see without changing what the server sees.

## How it works

China Hat is a client-side render only. It sits in the visual module group, which covers display and personalization options rather than anything that touches combat, movement, or world interaction. Modules in that group draw an extra element on top of the normal game view; the underlying game state, your position, your swings, your packets, stays exactly the same.

The name is the clearest clue to what it does: a hat-shaped model or icon layered into your view. Beyond that, there's no public documentation of the exact model, where it's anchored, or what triggers it to show. That's not unusual for a small cosmetic toggle bundled into a bigger visual settings page; it exists to be seen by the person using it, not to be reverse-engineered.

## Is it safe to use

Anticheats care about things that affect the game: illegal movement, extended reach, automated aim, timing patterns that don't match a human hand. A module that only draws a picture on your local screen doesn't produce any of that. It's functionally the same category of feature as a custom skin or a cape: a display choice, not an interaction with the game's rules.

That distinction is why cosmetic modules like this one rarely show up in anticheat writeups at all. There's nothing server-side to catch because there's nothing server-side happening.
