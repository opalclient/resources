Spider is a movement module that lets you climb straight up a solid wall as though it were a ladder bolted to the surface. Press toward stone, wood, dirt, whatever's in front of you, and instead of vanilla physics stopping you flat and letting gravity pull you back down, you rise up the face of the block. There's no ladder actually there. Minecraft has no legitimate way to do this, which is exactly why it's treated as a movement cheat rather than a mechanic, by anticheats and by server rules alike.

## How it works

Spider overrides the collision response Minecraft normally applies the moment you walk into a solid block. Vanilla physics only lets a small set of blocks, ladders, vines, scaffolding, apply an upward climbing force when you press into them. Everything else just stops you cold. Spider wires that same upward force to ordinary solid blocks instead, so pressing forward into a wall pushes you up its face rather than pinning you against it.

The result looks identical to climbing a real ladder: the motion, the speed, the feel, none of it changes. The only thing missing is the ladder itself.

## Why people use it

The appeal is skipping the setup. Reaching a roof, escaping a pit, or getting on top of a structure normally means placing blocks or hunting for stairs. Spider turns any wall into the shortcut instead, no building required.

It has a fight use too. Climbing a wall on command can break an opponent's line of sight or throw off their pathing faster than jumping and building ever would. That's part of why it shows up in movement-heavy clients like Tenacity, sitting alongside other modules built to speed up traversal.

## Is it safe to use

No, and the mechanism is exactly why not. Server-side anticheats track position and velocity every tick and compare the result against what vanilla physics allows. A player rising at a steady rate while pressed against a bare block, with no ladder, vine, or scaffolding anywhere near them, doesn't match any legitimate movement pattern. That mismatch is precisely the signal a movement check exists to catch.

What makes Spider more exposed than some other movement modules is how often the chance to see it comes up. It doesn't need a rare item or a specific setup: any wall in the world is a live test case for a server watching player movement. Treat it as use-at-your-own-risk, the same category as scaffold or killaura. It's entirely plausible for a server's anticheat to flag the behavior and act on it.
