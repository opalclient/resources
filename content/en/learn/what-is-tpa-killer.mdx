<KeyTakeaways>

- TPAKiller is an exploit module that auto-sends a teleport request, then attacks the instant the target lands.
- It strikes inside the brief window right after teleporting, while the target is still loading in and can barely react.
- It is not an aimbot or KillAura: the edge comes from timing and surprise, not combat automation.
- Documented as a module in the Tenacity client; servers close the exploit with spawn invulnerability or teleport rate limits.

</KeyTakeaways>

TPAKiller is an exploit module that automates one specific ambush: send a teleport request, wait for the target to accept, then attack the moment they land. It is built around a window most players never think about, the second or two right after a `/tpa` teleport when you are still rendering into the new area and have not regained full control. TPAKiller times its hit to land inside that gap, turning a routine social command into the setup for a free kill. It is documented as a module in the Tenacity client, and it sits squarely on the exploit side of the ledger, not the combat-automation side where something like an aimbot lives.

## How TPAKiller works

TPAKiller runs the whole sequence without the player doing anything by hand. It sends the teleport request (a `/tpa` command or equivalent) to a chosen target, watches for the acceptance, and fires an attack the instant the target lands next to the player. The target never sees it coming, because they agreed to a teleport, not a fight.

The exploit works because teleporting drops a player into a new position with no warning, and on a lot of servers there is a short gap before they can move or react normally. TPAKiller is built to act inside that exact gap, before the target has regained control or even finished loading the area in.

## Is TPAKiller safe to use

Running TPAKiller is not a gray area. Automating a social mechanic like a teleport request specifically to set up an ambush is a gameplay-advantage exploit: it wins fights the other player never agreed to and had no way to prevent by playing well. Most servers that offer `/tpa` expect it to be used for travel and grouping up, not as bait for a kill.

Detection is trickier here than for something like aim automation, because each individual action, sending a teleport request and then attacking, is completely legitimate on its own. What gives it away is the pattern: a teleport request followed by an attack with no meaningful delay, repeated across multiple targets, is a rhythm real players almost never produce by accident. That is also why the usual fix is not detection-based. A short invulnerability window after teleporting, or a rate limit on how often `/tpa` can be sent, removes the exploit before it needs catching at all.
