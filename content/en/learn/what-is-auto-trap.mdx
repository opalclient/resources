Auto Trap is a combat module that automatically boxes a target in with blocks the instant a fight opens, usually obsidian. It's a fixture of crystal and anchor PvP, where an opponent who can still move can dodge a crystal or step clear before a respawn anchor goes off. The player doesn't place a single block themselves. The module reads the fight, picks a target, and walls them in on its own, faster and cleaner than anyone building by hand.

## How Auto Trap works

Once Auto Trap has a nearby target, it starts placing blocks around them to close off the ways out. Obsidian is the default pick for a reason beyond how hard it is to break: it's also the block crystal PvP needs to place end crystals on, so the trap and the setup material are the same purchase. The module decides where every block goes and drops it without the player aiming, reaching, or clicking anything.

The trap itself does nothing offensive. Its only job is removing an opponent's ability to reposition. A free target can back off from a placed crystal or step outside an anchor's blast radius before it detonates. A boxed target can't do either, which is why Auto Trap exists purely as a setup step and never as the module that actually lands the hit.

## Is Auto Trap safe to use?

No. Boxing someone in without their consent is a real gameplay advantage, not a cosmetic one, and servers treat it as a cheat rather than a building shortcut. The placement itself is what gives it away: blocks going up around another player in a tight, exact sequence, often without the placing player's view ever turning toward the spot, isn't a pattern a person produces with a mouse.

Server-side anticheats that log placement speed, rotation, and how close a new block lands to another entity can flag a trap that closes faster or neater than manual building allows. And since the whole technique exists to feed crystal and anchor PvP, a server already watching for a Crystal Aura or Anchor Aura is usually watching for the same burst of rapid block placement that sets those combos up.

<Callout variant="tip" title="Where Opal draws the line">
Opal doesn't ship a dedicated Auto Trap. Automated block placement around another player is about as loud a signal as combat automation gets, placement speed, rotation, and proximity to an entity are exactly what a server-side anticheat is built to log. Opal's combat tools stay on the side of that line where the server can't tell the difference from someone who's just good at positioning.
</Callout>
