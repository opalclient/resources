Freecam is a Minecraft client module that splits your camera away from your character's body. Flip it on and the movement keys stop steering your player and start steering a free-floating viewpoint instead, while your actual character stays locked in place, or keeps doing whatever it was already doing. It's a rendering trick, not a movement one: the server never sees the camera move, only the body that isn't. Most people reach for it to scout a base from an angle they'd never risk walking into, or to pull a clean shot for a clip, not to fight or reposition faster.

## How Freecam works

Freecam separates the rendering camera from the player entity it's normally welded to. Once active, your usual movement keys steer that detached viewpoint through the world instead of your character, and the viewpoint has no hitbox and no collision. It flies through walls, floats over lava, whatever. Meanwhile your character stops exactly where you left it, or continues whatever it was already doing when you toggled the module.

That split matters because of what the server actually tracks. It only ever knows about your player entity's position, not your camera's, so nothing about your real location or hitbox changes while Freecam is active. The camera is a purely local, client-rendered thing that never touches the network. Tenacity, to name one, ships Freecam as a player-category module rather than bundling it under combat or movement, which is a fair reflection of what it actually does: nothing to your player.

Two use cases cover most of why people turn it on. Scouting: checking a base layout, peeking around a corner, or watching what someone else is doing without exposing or repositioning your actual character. Recording: pulling the camera back for a wide shot, following a fight from outside it, or lining up an angle for a clip, all without burning a second account or falling back on vanilla spectator mode, which brings its own problem: it changes your actual game mode, and the server logs that. Freecam doesn't touch your game mode at all.

## Is Freecam actually detectable

Freecam is a rendering change, not a movement or combat one, so it doesn't send extra packets tied to the camera's position. Your position packets stay tied to the character, and the character isn't moving, so a server generally has nothing unusual to observe while the camera is off wandering. That puts it well below modules like [KillAura](/en/learn/what-is-killaura) or [Reach](/en/learn/what-is-reach) on the risk scale, since those change what your player claims to be doing, and that claim is exactly what anticheats are built to catch.

Detection risk isn't the whole story, though. Some servers write rules against any camera or client modification, full stop, whether or not it's technically provable. Using Freecam to clear blind spots mid-fight can still count as an unfair advantage under those rules even if nothing ever flags you for it. Whether it's allowed comes down to server policy more than to what the packets say.

Freecam rarely trips an anticheat, because the server only ever sees your character's position, and your character hasn't gone anywhere. But detectable and allowed aren't the same thing. Plenty of rulesets ban any camera or client modification outright, and staff can still call it an unfair advantage in a screenshare even without a flag to point to. Check the rules before you lean on it competitively.
</Callout>

## FAQ

<FAQList>

<FAQItem question="Does using Freecam move your character?">
No. Your character stays exactly where it was when you turned Freecam on. Anything it does afterward comes from other inputs, not from moving the camera.
</FAQItem>

<FAQItem question="Can Freecam see through walls?">
Not on its own. The camera can pass through blocks and open space, but it still only renders whatever terrain and entities the server has already sent to your client, so Freecam alone will not reveal anything an ESP or fullbright module would.
</FAQItem>

<FAQItem question="Is Freecam the same as spectator mode?">
No. Vanilla spectator mode changes your actual game mode, and the server sees that change happen. Freecam is a client-only camera trick, so your character stays in whatever mode it was already in.
</FAQItem>

<FAQItem question="Is Freecam detectable by anticheats?">
Rarely. It does not send any packets tied to the camera's position, only to your character's, which has not moved. Some servers still restrict it by rule even where detecting it server-side is impractical.
</FAQItem>

<FAQItem question="Does every Minecraft utility client include Freecam?">
No, it is an optional module that each client implements on its own. Some ship it as a player-category module, others skip it entirely, so check a specific client's module list before assuming it is there.
</FAQItem>

</FAQList>
