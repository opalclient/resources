AutoHeadHitter is a movement module in Minecraft utility clients that automatically times a jump and adjusts your position so an attack lands on a mob's head hitbox instead of somewhere lower on its body. It's a farming tool, not a combat one: nothing about it aims at or damages other players. The mob most associated with it is the enderman, whose tall, narrow shape makes a clean head hit surprisingly hard to land by hand, over and over, for an hour straight. AutoHeadHitter takes the judgment call out of it, watching the target and your position every tick so headshots land automatically whenever a mob is in range.

## How it works

AutoHeadHitter watches the target mob and your own position on every tick, then decides when to jump and where to stand so the swing connects with the head instead of the body. Doing this by hand means judging the mob's height, the timing of the jump, and the angle of the swing all at once, and getting it right on hit after hit during a long session. The module removes that judgment call entirely. It triggers the jump and lines up the attack on its own whenever a valid target is in range.

Endermen are the mob this gets built around, and for good reason. Their tall, narrow hitbox punishes inconsistency: miss the head by a fraction and the hit either doesn't count as a headshot or doesn't land cleanly at all. Over hundreds of kills in a farming session, that adds up to real time lost, which is exactly the kind of repetitive precision task better handed to the client than to your reflexes.

## Is it safe to use

AutoHeadHitter targets mobs, not players, so it never touches the reach, rotation, or hit-timing checks anticheats build around player-versus-player combat. That puts it well below modules like [KillAura](/en/learn/what-is-killaura) or aim assistance on the risk ladder, since those manipulate combat directly against other people and get flagged specifically for it.

The remaining risk isn't about combat detection at all. It's about automation itself. Some servers ban any automated or macro-like behavior on principle, mob-targeting or not, on the theory that it's still the client acting instead of the player. Whether that matters on a given server comes down to that server's specific rules around automation, not whether an anticheat can single out headshot timing the way it singles out reach or rotation. Generally, it can't.
