<KeyTakeaways>

- KillAura Legit is a toned-down version of KillAura, built to resemble manual combat instead of maximizing hit rate.
- It caps range near melee distance, eases rotations over several ticks, and only attacks targets near your crosshair.
- Wurst ships it as a named module.
- Softer settings lower detection odds, not the automation itself, which anticheats are built to catch.

</KeyTakeaways>

KillAura Legit is a variant of KillAura built to look like a person swinging a sword instead of a script executing a loop. It runs the same scan-aim-attack cycle as regular KillAura, but every step is deliberately constrained: shorter range, slower rotations, narrower targeting. The goal isn't more kills, it's kills that don't look automated. That restraint is the entire premise, and also the entire limitation.

## How it works

KillAura Legit runs the same basic loop as KillAura: scan for a nearby entity, aim at it, send the attack. What changes is the leash on that loop. Range gets capped near vanilla melee distance instead of stretched past it, so the module can't reach anything a real player standing there couldn't touch. Rotations ease toward a target across several ticks rather than snapping to it instantly. Targeting is narrower too: the module only engages entities already close to where you're actually aiming, not anything within a generous radius around you.

That last constraint matters most. You still have to point roughly at an opponent for the module to act. It automates the fine aiming and the exact timing of a hit, not the decision of who to fight. That's the difference between "the client fights for you" and "the client fights a little better than you would have."

## Common settings

| Setting | What it controls |
|---|---|
| Range cap | Keeps attacks inside believable melee distance instead of extending past it |
| Rotation smoothing | Eases the view toward a target over several ticks instead of snapping to it |
| Crosshair proximity | Only attacks targets already near where you're aiming |
| Attack timing | Paces hits to the normal attack cooldown instead of firing as fast as possible |

Every one of these settings trades output for plausibility. Wide range and instant rotations land more hits, faster. Narrow range and smoothed rotations are the whole point of putting "legit" in the name, and they cost you speed to get there.

## Is it still detectable

Yes. Toning down the behavior makes KillAura Legit harder to catch than a raw KillAura, but it doesn't remove the underlying automation, and automation is exactly what anticheats are built to find. Real human aim drifts in small, inconsistent ways. A smoothed rotation curve can still look too even across a large enough sample of hits. Attacks paced precisely to the cooldown, repeated over time, also stand out against the natural variance of someone clicking a mouse.

The category is what matters, not the setting slider. KillAura Legit still decides when to attack and moves your view without your input, the same core behavior as KillAura. Softer settings lower the odds of a quick flag. They don't change what the module fundamentally is: combat automation, not a cosmetic tweak, and it carries the same rule violation as any other auto-attack module.
