Auto Fish is a Minecraft module that automates fishing: casting the rod, watching the bobber for a bite, and reeling in the catch without you tracking any of it yourself. It's documented as a player-category feature in Wurst, the same bucket of conveniences that trade manual repetition for a loop running quietly in the background. Vanilla fishing is slow by design: cast, wait, watch, click, repeat. Auto Fish exists to remove everything from that sequence except the part where you end up with fish, treasure, or an enchanted book.

## How it works

Auto Fish runs the same loop every time the rod hits the water: cast, watch for the splash or dip that signals a bite, reel in the instant that happens, cast again. The part that actually earns the name is the middle step. A player has to keep at least half an eye on the bobber and react by hand between whatever else they're doing; Auto Fish watches it constantly and reacts the moment the signal fires, which is exactly why it doesn't miss a bite and exactly why the timing ends up looking the way it does. Once the catch lands, the loop restarts with no re-cast, no re-watch, no attention required from anyone.

It ships in Wurst next to a handful of other player-category conveniences, automations built to cut down on repetitive manual tasks rather than to change how combat or movement actually plays out.

## Is it safe to use

Auto Fish sits near the bottom of the detection-risk scale, for one simple reason: fishing doesn't involve anyone but you. Casting and reeling are both ordinary, vanilla-legal actions. The module just performs them without a human watching the bobber, so there's no packet or interaction an anticheat can point to and call illegitimate.

The one thing that can stand out is timing. A bite answered in a near-identical interval every single time, session after session, is a pattern a server watching closely could flag as automated, even though the underlying action is completely normal. Most servers don't police fishing that tightly. Some AFK-focused servers ban automated reeling outright in their rules anyway, whether or not they can actually detect it, so it's worth a read through the rules before you leave a rig running overnight.

## FAQ

<FAQList>

<FAQItem question="Does Auto Fish give an advantage over other players?">
No. Fishing isn't a competition against anyone else, so Auto Fish doesn't change a PvP outcome or a race outcome. It only saves you from watching the bobber yourself.
</FAQItem>

<FAQItem question="Can servers detect Auto Fish?">
In principle, yes, since a server can watch how consistently you react to a bite. In practice, few servers police fishing automation the way they police combat or movement, so the real risk depends on that server's own rules.
</FAQItem>

<FAQItem question="Is Auto Fish the same as an autoclicker?">
Not really. A plain autoclicker fires on a timer with zero awareness of the game state. Auto Fish tracks the actual fishing state, cast, wait, bite, catch, so it handles the cast itself and knows exactly when a fish is on the line.
</FAQItem>

<FAQItem question="Does Auto Fish work while you're away from the keyboard?">
Yes, that's the whole point. It detects bites and reels in without any input from you, which is why it's the go-to for AFK fishing sessions.
</FAQItem>

</FAQList>
