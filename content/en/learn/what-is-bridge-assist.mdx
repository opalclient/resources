<KeyTakeaways>

- Bridge Assist is a movement module that auto-places blocks while you bridge by hand, correcting placement timing and angle.
- Light settings just smooth timing on a bridge you're already building.
- Heavy settings take over placement and aim, converging with Scaffold or God Bridge.
- Risk scales with the setting: heavy assist reads like Scaffold to anticheats watching placement rate and rotation.

</KeyTakeaways>

Bridge Assist is a movement module that auto-places blocks under you while you're bridging by hand, tracking your position and look direction each tick so placements land instead of missing the edge. You still supply the direction and the intent. You hold a block, walk forward, and place like you always do. The module's job is what happens in between: queuing the next block, nudging your angle toward the support square, retiming the swing and use inputs so the bridge doesn't break under you. It sits somewhere between fully manual bridging and a full Scaffold, and it shows up in clients like Slinky.

## How it works

Bridge Assist runs on top of ordinary manual bridging, not instead of it. You're already holding a block and walking forward, the same setup as bridging by hand. Each tick, the module reads your position and look direction and queues a placement just behind or below your feet as you advance. When a placement is due, it can nudge your look angle toward the support block and retime the swing and use inputs so the block actually connects instead of whiffing off the edge. Squares that are already filled get skipped, so the loop doesn't waste a placement or break the line of the bridge.

## Light vs heavy settings

How much of the bridging Bridge Assist is actually doing for you comes down to the setting.

| Setting | What it does | How it plays |
|---|---|---|
| Light assist | Fills in placements only while you hold a block and walk forward | Feels like normal bridging with the timing smoothed out |
| Heavy assist | Takes over more of the placement, angle correction, and timing | Starts to overlap with a full Scaffold or fast techniques like God Bridge |

At light strength you're still doing the bridging: direction, intent, and block choice are yours, the module just cleans up execution. At heavy strength it's doing more of the work your hands would otherwise be doing.

## Is it safe to use

Risk here tracks the setting, not just having the module installed. Light Bridge Assist corrects timing and angle on placements you're already making by walking and holding a block, which reads closer to a convenience feature than an automated cheat. Heavy Bridge Assist is a different story: it starts placing and aiming on your behalf, and at that point it's functionally the same thing as a full Scaffold or a fast technique like God Bridge. Anticheats that watch placement rate, rotation snapping, or blocks placed without a complete swing don't care what the module is called. Heavy Bridge Assist gets treated exactly like Scaffold.

<Callout variant="tip" title="Where Opal draws the line">
Opal doesn't ship a module called Bridge Assist, or anything that auto-places and auto-aims a bridge for you. Movement is very much part of what a utility client like Opal covers, but the automation that carries real ban risk isn't something worth building a module around.
</Callout>

<Callout variant="warning" title="Reference, not a how-to">
This page explains what the term means generically, not how to configure it. Heavy Bridge Assist settings function the same as a full Scaffold, which is bannable on essentially every server with an anticheat worth mentioning.
</Callout>
