### Ghost and blatant in one client

Most clients make you choose. Ghost clients give you subtle features but no bypasses. Blatant clients give you bypasses but no ghost mode. Opal does both, so you can run closet aim assist or full kill aura depending on the situation.

### A platform, not just a JAR

Other clients hand you a file and a Discord invite. Opal comes with more:

- A desktop launcher that handles install, login, and updates.
- A web dashboard for your account, HWID resets, downloads, and changelogs.
- A config browser to find and share module presets.
- Community forums where bug reports and feature requests shape the roadmap.
- A Discord bot for license management and support.

### GraalVM scripting engine

Opal ships a full JavaScript runtime. You can write scripts that talk to the client's APIs, automate tasks, and build your own modules. It is GraalVM, the same engine used in enterprise Java, running inside Minecraft. Not a macro system. See [the scripting engine writeup](/en/blog/introducing-opal-scripting-engine).

### Cross-platform

Windows, macOS on Intel and Apple Silicon, and Linux. You do not have to pick an operating system.

## Why so cheap?

Honest answer: Opal is the entry point to our ecosystem. At $8.99 the barrier to trying it is almost nothing, and we want as many players as possible to feel what a premium client is like. When you want more, like multi-anticheat support and additional game modes, Terminus is the upgrade.

Opal is not a demo, though. It is a full client that stands on its own, and plenty of users never need anything beyond it.
