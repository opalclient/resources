Shaders always cost some frames, but you can keep them playable. The foundation is [Iris](https://modrinth.com/mod/iris) for shaders running on top of [Sodium](https://modrinth.com/mod/sodium) for performance, which is far faster than the old way. Then you pick a lighter pack and trim the most expensive effects. Both Iris and Sodium run alongside Opal, so a shader-lit game and a full client live on the same setup.

<KeyTakeaways>

- Iris on top of Sodium is the single most important step for playable shaders.
- A lighter pack and trimmed effects recover most of the lost frames.
- Iris and Sodium both run with Opal; only Lithium is incompatible.
- Keep render distance moderate; shaders multiply its cost.

</KeyTakeaways>

## Step 1: Use Iris with Sodium

<Steps>

<Step number={1} title="Install Sodium">
[Sodium](https://modrinth.com/mod/sodium) is the fast renderer that does the heavy lifting. Put the [Fabric](https://fabricmc.net) loader and [Fabric API](https://modrinth.com/mod/fabric-api) in first, then drop Sodium in your `mods` folder. It runs fine alongside Opal.
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Add Iris">
[Iris](https://modrinth.com/mod/iris) runs shader packs and is built to sit on top of Sodium, so you keep the framerate you just gained. This combination is the whole trick.
</Step>

</Steps>

## Step 2: Pick a lighter pack

Heavy realistic packs cost the most. A balanced pack, or a low profile of a heavier one, recovers a lot of frames while keeping most of the look.

## Step 3: Trim the expensive effects

Inside the shader's settings, the big costs are usually:

<Checklist>
<ChecklistItem>Volumetric lighting and fog.</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem>Reflections, screen-space or water.</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem>Shadow resolution and distance.</ChecklistItem>
</Checklist>

Lowering these keeps most of the look for a fraction of the cost.

## Step 4: Mind render distance

Shaders multiply the cost of every chunk drawn, so a high render distance hurts twice. Keep it moderate.

<Callout variant="tip" title="Opal users">
Sodium and Iris both run with Opal, so you can have shaders and the client at once. Lithium is the only common mod that does not work with Opal, so leave it out and let Sodium handle performance.
</Callout>

## Where Opal fits

Opal is a Fabric mod for Minecraft 1.21.10 and installs into the same `mods` folder as Sodium and Iris. You get a shader-lit world plus a full client: 200+ features, a glass UI, and a scripting engine. The glass interface is built to look right over a shader-lit game, so the two complement each other rather than fight. Keep Sodium in for headroom and leave Lithium out.
