Multiplayer adds work a single-player world does not: network packets, more players to render, and chunks streaming in as you move. Client-side, [Krypton](https://modrinth.com/mod/krypton) optimizes networking, [Sodium](https://modrinth.com/mod/sodium) keeps rendering fast under a crowd, and **C2ME** smooths chunk loading. None of these fix a slow server, but they make your side of the connection as smooth as it can be. One note for Opal users: Lithium is not compatible with Opal, so build around Sodium.

<KeyTakeaways>

- Sodium holds your framerate when the screen is full of players and particles.
- Krypton trims networking overhead on busy servers.
- C2ME speeds up chunk loading so fast travel hitches less.
- Client mods cannot fix server lag. Lithium is not compatible with Opal.

</KeyTakeaways>

## Krypton

Optimizes Minecraft's networking stack on the client. It reduces overhead in how packets are handled, which helps when a busy server is sending a lot of updates.

## Sodium

Keeps your frame rate stable when there are many players, entities, and particles on screen at once, which is exactly when vanilla rendering struggles. It runs fine alongside Opal.

## C2ME

Speeds up chunk loading so moving fast across a large multiplayer map hitches less.

## What mods cannot fix

If blocks rubber-band and mobs teleport, that is server-side lag or your connection, not your client. Use a wired link, pick a nearby region, and close bandwidth-heavy apps. We would rather tell you that than sell you a mod that cannot deliver it.

<Callout variant="warning" title="Running Opal? Skip Lithium">
Lithium is a common multiplayer pick, but it is not compatible with Opal. Run Sodium and Krypton, and leave Lithium out.
</Callout>
