On Fabric the clean way to run shaders is [Iris](https://modrinth.com/mod/iris), which loads shader packs and is built to run with [Sodium](https://modrinth.com/mod/sodium) so you keep your framerate. Install the two mods, drop a pack in a folder, pick it in video settings. That is the whole job. No OptiFine required.

<KeyTakeaways>

- Iris runs shaders on Fabric, no OptiFine needed.
- Pair Iris with Sodium so shaders cost you fewer frames.
- Shader packs live in the `shaderpacks` folder; pick one in video settings.
- Opal runs in the same folder, so you can have shaders and a full client.

</KeyTakeaways>

## Install shaders

<Steps>

<Step number={1} title="Install Iris and Sodium">
Install the [Fabric](https://fabricmc.net) loader and the [Fabric API](https://modrinth.com/mod/fabric-api), then add [Iris](https://modrinth.com/mod/iris) and [Sodium](https://modrinth.com/mod/sodium) to your `mods` folder. Iris runs the shaders, Sodium keeps the FPS up. They are designed to work together.
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Add a shader pack">
Download a shader pack (it comes as a single file) and put it in the `shaderpacks` folder inside your Minecraft directory. If that folder is not there yet, launch the game once with Iris installed and it gets created for you.
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Select it in game">
Open Video Settings, then the shader packs screen, and pick the pack you added. It applies right away, and you can swap packs from the same screen.
</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout variant="warning" title="If your FPS drops too far">
Lower your render distance or switch to a lighter pack before blaming the mods. Heavy shaders cost real frames, and a couple of chunks of render distance often buys back what you need.
</Callout>

## Picking a pack

<Checklist>
<ChecklistItem>Lighter packs cost less FPS and still look good. Start there if your machine is modest.</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem>Heavier packs add fancy water, shadows, and volumetric lighting, at a real frame cost.</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem>Match the pack to your GPU, not to the screenshots.</ChecklistItem>
</Checklist>

## Running shaders with a client

Because Iris and Sodium are Fabric mods, anything else built as a Fabric mod shares the folder, including Opal. So a shader-lit game and a full utility client are not an either-or. Opal targets Minecraft 1.21.10 and loads next to your shader stack, so you can run the looks and the feature set together.
