Quality-of-life mods do not change the game so much as remove its small frustrations. [ModMenu](https://modrinth.com/mod/modmenu) gives you a mod list and config screens, [AppleSkin](https://modrinth.com/mod/appleskin) shows hunger you cannot normally see, [Mouse Tweaks](https://modrinth.com/mod/mouse-tweaks) speeds up inventories, and [Jade](https://modrinth.com/mod/jade) tells you what you are looking at. Install these and everyday play feels smoother. They all run alongside a client like Opal too.

<KeyTakeaways>

- ModMenu is the base layer; install it first to manage the rest.
- AppleSkin, Mouse Tweaks, Jade, and Controlling each remove a small friction.
- All are light and run alongside Opal in the same Fabric mods folder.
- Leave Lithium out if you run Opal; it is the one common incompatibility.

</KeyTakeaways>

## ModMenu

Adds the in-game mod list and config screens, so you can see what is installed and change settings without editing files. The base layer of any good [Fabric](https://fabricmc.net) setup.

## AppleSkin

Shows the saturation and hunger values the vanilla HUD hides, plus how much a food restores before you eat it. Quietly useful every session.

## Mouse Tweaks

Speeds up inventory management with click-drag moving, scroll transfer, and quick stacking. Once you have it, sorting chests without it feels slow.

## Jade

A "what am I looking at" overlay that names the block or mob under your crosshair and shows useful details. Great for learning and for modpacks.

## Controlling

Adds a search box to the controls menu and flags conflicting keybinds, which is a lifesaver once you have a lot bound to keys, including a client's keybinds.

## Where a client fits

These mods polish vanilla survival play. Opal is a different layer: a Fabric mod for Minecraft 1.21.10 with 200+ combat, movement, visual, and utility features and a glass UI, plus its own in-game HUD readouts and config. It does not replace survival QoL mods like AppleSkin or Jade, and they run happily alongside it. Install the QoL set for everyday smoothness, add Opal for the feature set, and keep Lithium out of the folder.
